Growing up,my mother always said, "You're such a diva", and I always thought it was an insult. So naturally, I began to personalize it. To me, a diva is a confident, ethereal, and kind woman who gets what she want's and is humble about her successes.

Catholicism was such a big thing in my family; I believed that there was heaven and hell and that the devil was going to get me if I didn’t go to sleep at a certain time. My father and mother were immigrants from two very different countries: Ghana and the Philippines. I can’t speak either of their languages due to their fear of my falling behind. My mother hated how much of a homebody my father was. She made sure I saw the world through many different perspectives. My exploration of the world from an early age has shaped me into the person I am right now. My curiosity is my biggest strength and my biggest weakness.
